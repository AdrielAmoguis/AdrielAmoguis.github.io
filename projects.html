Personal - CussNet

CussNet (tentative name) is one of my upcoming projects for 2021. It is a Neural Network that aims to identify text profanity in the context of both English and Filipino languages. There are already several libraries available for free for any development environment that can identify profanity in the English context. However, as the global pandemic forces us indoors, several communities have taken their social sphere online -- the Philippines is not exception to this.

With the rapidly growing internet users from the Philippines, CussNet aims to help online communities automate their moderation workflow to keep online Filipino communities a safe-space and free from text profanity.

Lasallian Youth Orchestra - Antonio

Antonio is the unofficial mascot of the Lasallian Youth Orchestra. He is the face (quite literally the profile photo) of our official Discord bot. This Discord bot is responsible for member interaction while the LYO Google Services Integration (headed by Lorenzo Querol) is still under development. This is where they see their account details, Discord voice hours, and many more organization-specific details.

Being an organization under the Culture & Arts Office (CAO), one of the requirements for a trainee to become a full member of the orchestra is residency. In pre-pandemic times, residency simply meant staying in the rehearsal hall for a set amount of hours for two terms. The rehearsal hall has a computer with a small Java Swing application on it that tracks everyone's residency time by simply scanning a person's DLSU ID as they go in and go out of the hall.

Now being in the online setting, we needed a similar system that would automatically track trainees' number of hours in our 'virtual rehearsal hall' which is a Discord server. Antonio was specifically made for this purpose. A fully automated bot to track voice activity (but no recording) to calculate residency hours. The other features of Antonio were just developed as side-features that could potentially aid the members' quality of interaction on the server.

Antonio was built using the Discord.py Library for Python. It uses the LYO DataAPI to communicate with LYO's database. Deployed with Docker.

Lasallian Youth Orchestra - DataAPI

The Lasallian Youth Orchestra DataAPI is responsible for handling the organization's data from MongoDB to the different LYO services that need it (Antonio, LYO Members' Portal, LYO Public Website).

This API-layer ensures that data is only passed on to entities or clients that are authorized to handle such data or execute operations. Apart from the authentication method we use (which will be confidential for security reasons), the API also has a permissions-system to ensure that data access and flow is controlled. Different types of user accounts can only access data that is prescribed for them to access.

To date, the API layer runs on an undisclosed domain and port and secured with SSL. Deployed with Docker.

Lasallian Youth Orchestra - Members' Portal

The Lasallian Youth Orchestra requires services and workflows that are tailored specifically for its nature. A lot of these workflows involve CAO A&A grading, artist profiling, among others. As of the time being, LYO relies heavily on the services that Google provides. We hope to create a streamlined process and workflow to help LYO officers and members stay in touch with their requirements, A&A grading, profiling, and paperwork. We aim to integrate multiple types of services to this project, namely:

  • Sched-It Calendar & Appointment System (CCAPDEV S11-G44 Project)
    • This will be used a lot during recruitment week to schedule appointments with potential members.
  • User Account System to Track Member A&A Grades, Academic Standing, & Other Details Used for CAO Paperwork (Just like Animo.Sys)
    • The Culture & Arts Office requires paperwork about the organization and its members every term. Our goal is to automate this.
  • Canvas-like Service for Members' Music Requirements
  • Other features we have yet to decide on

This is the most ambitious of all LYO's development team projects. Development on this has not yet begun. The team plans to use ReactJS with the LYO DataAPI Layer.

Estimated month of completion: November-December 2021
